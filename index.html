About

I am a project researcher at Kavli IPMU, working at the interface of . My recent work explores hybrid generative machine learning for inferring masses of heavy underlying particles over a wide parameter space and reconstructing 'truth' parton-level phase space (Unfolding) from collider data

Research Interests

Collider ML

Generative models (diffusion/normalizing flows) and transformers for parameter and state reconstruction in hadron collider environments.

Top Quark

Modern ML for top reconstruction and tagging; implications for EFT and non-standard interactions; performance and systematics.

BSM & DM

Singlino-dominated neutralino dark matter in the NMSSM; resonant annihilation and co-annihilation corridors vs. current constraints.

Electroweak

Precision probes of electroweak gauge interactions (e.g., γjj) with likelihood-free inference and sequence models.

Selected Highlights

Hybrid generative + transformer model for mass reconstruction — reconstructs heavy-particle masses from detector-level features over a wide parameter space, outperforming classical methods. [preprint / code links]
Survey of ML techniques for top-quark reconstruction — ~10–15% improvements in tagging/reco metrics across benchmarks; sensitivity studies for non-standard interactions. [review / repo]
NMSSM singlino dark matter parameter space — resonant-Higgs and co-annihilation regimes consistent with relic density and current LHC/DD bounds. [paper / plots]
HL-LHC reach for lepton-number-violation signals in radiative ν-mass models — identifies new discovery windows beyond Run‑2 sensitivity. [work in progress]

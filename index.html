Rahool K. Barman

Assistant Professor
Department of Physics
IIT Madras

Rahool is a particle phenomenologist and Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics at IIT Madras, working on theoretical models of elementary particles and their experimental tests. His group focuses on theories that address the open questions in the Standard Model of particle physics, and develops strategies to probe them at ongoing and future experiments, including the Large Hadron Collider and upcoming hadron and lepton colliders, through simulation-based analyses.

A key interest of his group is developing and implementing physics-informed machine learning strategies to boost the discovery potential of beyond the Standard Model (BSM) phenomena at the high-energy colliders. Beyond colliders, they study inputs from dark matter searches, precision low-energy data, and neutrino oscillation measurements to constrain BSM models and to better understand where the new physics might be hiding.

Research Focus

The Standard Model (SM) of particle physics has been remarkably successful in describing the observed elementary particles and their interactions, but it leaves several key questions unanswered, such as the nature of dark matter, the origin of neutrino masses, matter-antimatter asymmetry, and the hierarchy problem. These open questions motivate the search for new physics beyond the Standard Model. With the upcoming high luminosity run of the LHC (HL-LHC) and prospective lepton colliders on the horizon, particle physics is entering a data-rich era, which will allow studying processes that are currently rare and measuring deviations from the SM with far greater precision. Additionally, advances in dark matter searches, astrophysical measurements, and low-energy precision data are likely to unravel new parameter spaces. Leveraging these opportunities, our research aims to contiribute in the pursuit of physics beyond the Standard Model, by focusing on the following three broad directions:

Probing beyond the Standard Model signatures at colliders

We perform simulation-based collider studies to test well-motivated new physics scenarios at current and future colliders, focusing on BSM Higgs, top quark and electroweak sectors, exotic particle searches, and interpretations within the Standard Model Effective Field Theory framework. We also study signatures of lepton number violation, particularly interactions with ΔL = 2, which would indicate that neutrinos are Majorana in nature. Such interactions, if present at the 𝒪(TeV) scale, may lead to characteristic collider signatures, such as same-sign dilepton plus jets. Observing such events would be strong evidence for physics beyond the Standard Model.

Machine Learning-driven collider search strategies

We investigate physics-informed machine learning (ML) strategies as a tool to improve event reconstruction, particle identification, likelihood-free inference, and background suppression, with the ultimate goal of improving the sensitivity of rare and subtle new physics signatures at the colliders. In previous works, we have explored generative models for unfolding detector-level events to the `truth' hard-scattering level, hybrid models for mass reconstruction, and likelihood-free inference approaches.

Combining insights across collider, astrophysical, and low-energy measurements

New physics models are being increasingly constrained and steered by inputs from multiple experiments. We study the interplay between collider searches, dark matter probes, neutrino data, and low-energy measurements, to identify viable parameter spaces in different new physics models and investigate their prospects at future experiments.

About

My research area is High Energy Physics (HEP) Phenomenology. I study the signatures of physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM) at the LHC and the upcoming high-energy colliders, with a major focus on extended scalar sectors and anomalous top quark interactions. More recently, I've been exploring Machine Learning (ML) methods, including generative models, attention networks and likelihood-free inference, for event reconstruction, high-dimensional unfolding, and fast EFT interpretation. I'm also interested in Dark Matter (DM) phenomenology. A recurring focus of my work has been on studying the complementarity, where we combine inputs from multiple avenues, including collider searches, dark mater probes, low energy measurements, to identify viable parameter space and develop strategies to probe them at future experiments.
